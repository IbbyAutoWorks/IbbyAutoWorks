# Ibby Auto Works™

Mobile auto service with a customer-first shop experience.

Ibby Auto Works™ is being shaped as a mobile auto repair and service brand for drivers who want clear communication, practical diagnostics, and a cleaner way to request help without chasing phone calls or paperwork.

This repository is the business and visual-reference home for the brand. It explains what Ibby Auto Works is, how the service should feel to customers, and the design direction behind the public app experience.

## What Ibby Auto Works does

Ibby Auto Works is built around mobile, appointment-based vehicle support. The service direction includes:

- mobile diagnostics and quick inspections
- battery, brake, electrical, and common repair support
- customer-submitted service requests
- vehicle records and service history
- photos, notes, estimates, and follow-up communication
- payment links, promotions, and repair-plan options as the business grows

The goal is simple: make car trouble feel less chaotic.

## Customer promise

Ibby Auto Works should feel:

- clear — customers know what is happening and why
- practical — recommendations are grounded in real vehicle symptoms
- respectful — no pressure, no confusing shop talk, no hidden runaround
- mobile-first — request service from a phone and keep records in one place
- reliable — updates, reminders, and repair history should be easy to find

## Service experience

A customer should be able to:

1. describe the vehicle issue in plain language
2. share contact and vehicle details
3. request mobile service or inspection
4. receive updates, photos, notes, and recommendations
5. review estimates or payment links
6. keep service records tied to their account

The app exists to support that experience — not to make the customer learn software.

## Brand direction

Ibby Auto Works should visually land somewhere between:

- clean mobile mechanic service
- trustworthy local repair shop
- modern customer portal
- slightly rugged, tool-ready automotive identity

Design should avoid feeling like a generic SaaS template. It should look like a real service business customers can trust with their car.
